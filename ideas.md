# Content Ideas

Suggested new content for the Seattle Urbanism Guide, organized by priority.

## Tier 1: New Standalone Guides (High Priority)

### Housing Production, Zoning Reform & the One Seattle Plan
The single biggest content gap. No existing guide covers the end-to-end story of Seattle's zoning overhaul. Topics to cover:
- **HB 1110 / HB 1337 implementation:** What changed, what's now legal on residential lots (4 units by-right, 6 near transit), ADU rule updates (no owner-occupancy, no parking, two ADUs allowed)
- **One Seattle Plan phased rollout:** Phase 1 (effective January 2026), Phase 2 Centers & Corridors (transmitted January 2026), Phase 3 Regional Growth Centers (2026-2028)
- **Design review reform:** Moratorium (October 2025), permanent reform replacing 8 boards with 1 citywide board
- **Affordable housing pipeline:** $155M/20 buildings/2,116 homes in 2025; Seattle leading nation in affordable apartment production; MHA revenue decline ($74M in 2021 to ~$22M in 2025-2026)
- **Seattle Social Housing Developer:** I-135 creation, Proposition 1A funding, $115M Council vote (February 2026), 2,000-unit target
- **State legislative context:** HB 1217 rent stabilization, Housing Trust Fund (~$600M), lot splitting (HB 1096), condo reform (HB 2304), factory-built housing (HB 2151)
- **Production data vs. zoning capacity:** The gap between what's legal and what's getting built
- Key sources: seattle.gov/buildingconnections, theurbanist.org, council.seattle.gov, SDCI permit data

### Homelessness and Shelter Policy
A contested, high-profile topic deeply connected to housing, transit, public space, and city budgets. Topics to cover:
- **KCRHA structure and role:** Regional governance model, funding, accountability
- **Shelter types and capacity:** Enhanced shelters, tiny house villages, Tent Cities, hotel conversions; gap between beds and population
- **Legal landscape:** *Grants Pass v. City of Grants Pass* (2024 Supreme Court ruling), WA state HB 2489 (restricting sweeps without adequate shelter alternatives)
- **Mayor Wilson's approach:** Paused sweeps, executive orders on shelter expansion
- **Funding:** $104.6M KCRHA budget (2025), $342-343M/yr affordable housing, federal Emergency Housing Voucher sunset (2026)
- **Connection to housing production:** How shelter, transitional housing, and permanent supportive housing fit into the broader housing spectrum
- Key sources: seattle.gov homelessness action plan, kcrha.org, theurbanist.org, publicola.com

### Industrial Lands, Maritime Policy & Freight
No existing guide covers industrial zoning, the Port of Seattle, or the housing-vs-industry tension. Topics to cover:
- **New three-zone MIC framework:** Maritime, Manufacturing and Logistics (MML); Industry and Innovation (II); Urban Industrial (UI) -- adopted as part of the comprehensive plan
- **Industrial lands by the numbers:** 12% of Seattle's land, 30% of revenue, 35,000 projected new jobs over 20 years
- **Port of Seattle:** $1.1B operating / $4.4B five-year capital budget, Northwest Seaport Alliance, "Teal New Deal" clean energy vision
- **Stadium district controversy:** March 2025 Council rezone to allow housing, invalidated November 2025 after Port/BNSF appeal
- **Duwamish Valley:** Environmental justice intersection, Greater Duwamish MIC, Superfund site adjacency
- **BINMIC (Ballard Interbay):** Industrial preservation vs. transit-oriented development pressure
- Key sources: seattle.gov/opcd industrial strategy, portseattle.org, theurbanist.org, realchangenews.org

## Tier 3: New Glossary Entries

### Housing & Zoning (`housing-zoning.md`)
- **One Seattle Plan** -- Seattle's 2024 comprehensive plan update and phased zoning implementation
- **Seattle Social Housing Developer (SSHD)** -- Public development authority created by I-135 to build permanently affordable, publicly owned housing
- **Proposition 1A** -- 2025 ballot measure funding the Social Housing Developer via Excess Compensation Tax
- **Design Review** -- City process for evaluating development proposals; reformed 2025 from 8 boards to 1

### Transportation (`transportation.md`)
- **HB 1491** -- 2025 WA state law requiring dense, mixed-use zoning near transit stations (compliance by December 2029)

### Land Use & Planning (`land-use.md`)
- **Manufacturing Industrial Center (MIC)** -- State-designated industrial areas (Greater Duwamish, BINMIC) with special zoning protections
- **MML / II / UI Zones** -- Seattle's new three-tier industrial zoning framework replacing the previous IG and IB zones
- **KCRHA** -- King County Regional Homelessness Authority; regional governance body for homelessness services

### Funding & Policy (`funding-policy.md`)
- **System Development Charges (SDCs)** -- One-time fees on new development to fund water, sewer, and drainage infrastructure (effective January 2026)
- **Housing Trust Fund** -- Washington state's primary affordable housing capital fund (~$600M in 2025 session)
- **Green Gentrification** -- When environmental improvements (parks, green infrastructure) increase property values and displace low-income residents
- **Excess Compensation Tax** -- Seattle payroll tax on high earners funding social housing (Proposition 1A)

## Tier 5: Blog Post Ideas

### Schools, Enrollment, and Housing: What Closing 21 Schools Tells Us About Seattle
SPS proposed closing 21 elementary schools (later 4, then none) due to 65% capacity and a $104M deficit. Analyze the tension between declining enrollment and new housing production. Does building more housing bring families? Key sources: SPS Well-Resourced Schools data, SDCI permit data by neighborhood, Census ACS.

### Cultural Displacement: Where Did Seattle's Arts Spaces Go?
Track the loss of arts and cultural spaces in Capitol Hill, Georgetown, and Pioneer Square. Cover the Cultural Overlay District Advisory Committee model, the city's 2025-2030 Arts Strategic Plan, and the Admissions Tax funding stream. Key sources: seattle.gov/arts, artbeat.seattle.gov.

### Food Access After Federal Cuts
The city's $12M food access investment, Food Action Plan, Fresh Bucks program, and 14 community gardens / 16 orchards / 2 urban farms -- contextualized against federal nutrition program cuts. Key sources: seattle.gov Food Action Plan, Food Equity Fund, Mayor's budget proposal.

### The One Seattle Plan Zoning Maps, Explained
Visual walkthrough of what Phase 1 and Phase 2 actually change on the ground. Where are the new Neighborhood Centers? How deep are corridor rezones? What got trimmed under neighborhood pressure? Key sources: One Seattle Plan Zoning Map (ArcGIS), OPCD corridor proposals, The Urbanist coverage.

### How the CSO Tunnel Will (Eventually) Clean Up Seattle's Waterways
The $700M+ Ship Canal combined sewer overflow project, Seattle's combined sewer system (33% of network), and what it means for water quality and climate adaptation. Key sources: SPU Overflow Action Plan, 700milliongallons.org.
